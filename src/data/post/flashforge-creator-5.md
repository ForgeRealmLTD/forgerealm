There has been a proper debate going on in the 3D printing community for a few months now. Two new machines were announced before either of them officially shipped, and people have been picking sides. On one end you have the Snapmaker UI. On the other, the Flashforge Creator 5. Both are serious printers aimed at people who want to move beyond hobbyist-tier hardware, and the people arguing about them are not casual users, they are experienced print farm operators, independent vendors, people who have been doing this for years and know what they are talking about.

We have met quite a few of those people at our stalls. When you set up at Albion Place or Kirkgate regularly, you start running into the same faces. Some of them have been doing markets for five or six years, running multiple printers, managing stock at a scale we are still working towards. They gave us genuine, useful advice on both machines. And most of them, for what it is worth, ended up landing on the Snapmaker UI.

We went the other way.

## Why the Creator 5

Our entire print farm runs on Flashforge hardware. The reliability has been consistently good and when something has gone wrong it has been diagnosable and fixable without drama. Switching to a completely different ecosystem at this stage, with a new printer from a brand we have less history with, felt like unnecessary risk when the alternative is a newer, more capable machine from the manufacturer we already trust.

We are also not trying to be the next anybody. The experienced traders gave us good advice and we took what was useful from it, but ForgeRealm has its own direction and part of that is making decisions that fit our setup rather than following the consensus. The consensus may well be right about the Snapmaker UI. It looks like a strong machine and the people who chose it know what they are doing. We just made a different call.

## What comes next

The Creator 5 is booked and we are expecting delivery at the start of May. When it arrives we will run it in properly before it touches production stock, same as we have done with every other printer. If it performs the way we expect, it changes what we can offer in terms of volume and material range, both of which are currently the main limits on what we can take to a stall.

More on this once it is actually in the studio.
